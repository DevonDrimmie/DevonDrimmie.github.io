Introduction

Welcome to my third, and final, work term report. My name is Devon Miller and I recently completed an 8 month term working for Sun Life Financial in their Ignite Innovation Studio. I was designated an Innovation developer, which I will describe in more detail later on.

Throughout this website you will find a description of my duties, my learning goals, and what I feel I gained from the experience. Read on to learn the day to day life of an innovation developer at Sun Life, I hope you find this information interesting!

About The Job

About the Employer

Sun Life Finanical is a Canadian Finance Company that specializes in Life Insurance. It operates in over 12 countries, and is one of the largest life insurance providers in the world. They have recently begun investing heavily into technical development to help stay competitive.

One of the investments they made was creating their Ignite Studio Innovation Center in Downtown Toronto, where I worked. It was an open, agile space that provided state of the art tools for rapid prototyping and innovation. It was meant to mimic the feel of a Start-Up, without the lack of resources that usually come with a Start-Up.

Goals

I knew I had an amazing Oppurtunity, so I focused myself into 4 Learning Goals, and put my energy into developing them the best that I could.

Improve Presentation Skills: Working in an agile enviornment, I was required to demo my completed stories at the end of each week. This was an issue considering I would normally get nervous speaking infront of others. I took this oppurtunity to practice as much as I could, and by the end of the term I no longer felt nervous

Improve Leadership Skills: At the end of my first 4 month term, all of my fellow co-ops left, and a new co-hort arrived. This put me in a position where I held seniority, and was required to lead the other Co-ops. I felt I should take this seriously, and work on developing leadership skills as best I could.

Learn Node.js: Coming into this position, I had never worked with Node.js, or any JavaScript for that matter. I was hoping that by the end of the term, I would feel competent with these technologies. I feel that I succeeded in this regard.

Job Description

When applying for this position, the job was described as follows:

"The Innovation Web Application Developer Coop will be an entry level member of a team that provides development and maintenance efforts on a variety of web and mobile based application projects in our Innovation Program. The Coop will utilize his/her keen interest, enthusiasm and experience to produce prototype deliverables in a cost-effective and timely manner through our Innovation Program."

This may seem vague, but that was due to the fact that the innovation program is encouraged to come up with ideas of their own, and prototype them. The job became whatever we could make of it.

This level of freedom turned out to be daunting, but a huge asset. Once we felt comfortable we were producing new product prototypes on a bi-weekly basis.

Conclusion

Altogether, this has been the greatest work term I have completed thus far, for a number of reasons. Firstly, it would be hard to match the personal development I made here, with regards to technical skills and interpersonal skills. Secondly, working in the heart of downtown toronto, in a flashy new office, the enviornment was also hard to beat. The job was hard, but I loved every minute of it.
